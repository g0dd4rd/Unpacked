19 - Geometry in music

In this chapter, we will discover that music and its elements have shapes and thus contains geometry.

Chords, triads and arpeggios shapes

Major triad

The major triad forms a triangle shape in any key, in this case it's E major triad / chord.

Major seventh arpeggio

The major seventh arpeggio forms a trapezoid in any key, in this cas it's E major seventh arpeggio / chord.

Dominant seventh arpeggio

The dominant seventh arpeggio / chord forms a trapezoid shape in any key, in this case it's E dominant seventh arpeggio / chord.

Minor triad

The minor triad forms a isosceles triangle shape in any key, in this case it's E minor triad / chord.

Minor seventh arpeggio

The minor seventh arpeggio forms a isosceles trapezoid in any key, in this cas it's E minor seventh arpeggio / chord.

Diminished triad

The diminished triad forms a equilateral triangle shape in any key, in this case it's E diminished triad / chord.

Half-diminished seventh arpeggio

The half-diminished arpeggio forms almost a diamond shape in any key, in this case it's E half-diminished triad / chord.

Diminished seventh arpeggio

The diminished arpeggio forms a diamond shape in any key, in this case it's E diminished triad / chord.

Augmented triad

The augmented triad forms a equilateral triangle shape in any key, in this case it's E augmented triad / chord.

Augmented seventh arpeggio

The augmented seventh arpeggio forms a irregular trapezoid shape in any key, in this case it's E augmented arepggio / chord.

Minor-major seventh arpeggio

The Minor-major seventh arpeggio forms a irregular trapezoid shape in any key, in this case it's E Minor-major arepggio / chord.

Chord progressions and their shapes

I-IV-V

This is how a regular I-IV-V chord progression looks like, in the key of E natural major.

ii-V-I

This is how a regular ii-V-I chord progression looks like, in the key of E natural major.

The standard tuning

The standard tuning in any key forms a pentagram.

Scales shapes

The natural major scale - ionian mode

The natural major scale also called the ionian mode, has a shape of irregular septagon.

Dorian mode

The dorian mode has a shape of a regular, symmetric septagon.

Phrygian mode

The phrygian mode has a shape of a irregular septagon, that is actually the same as ionian mode in the same key, just rotated.

Lydian mode

The lydian mode has a shape of a irregular septagon, that is actually the same as ionian mode in the same key, just rotated.

Mixolydian mode

The mixolydian mode has a shape of a irregular septagon, that is actually the same as ionian mode in the same key, just rotated.

Aeolian mode

The aeolian mode has a shape of a irregular septagon, that is actually the same as myxolydian mode in the same key, but vertically mirrored.

Locrian mode

The locrian mode has a shape of a irregular septagon, that is actually the same as lydian mode in the same key, but vertically mirrored.
